AUSTRIA

Vienna

Vienna is the capital city of Austria.

Schonbrunn Palace

The majestic Schonbrunn Palace is located on the outskirts of Vienna. It was the primary summer residence of the Hapsburg monarchy.

Saint Stephen's Cathedral

This large cathedral was built in the 1300s, and has a mesmerizing colorful roof.

Soviet War Memorial

The Heldendenkmal der Roten Armee (War Memorial to the Red Army) is a tall monument dedicated to the 17,000 Soviet troops that perished in the Vienna Offensive in World War 2. At the end of the war, Vienna was partitioned similarly to Berlin between the four victorious Allied powers (UK, USA, USSR, and France). Because Austria declared its permanent neutrality in the Austrian State Treaty in 1955, the USSR and the other Allied powers all withdrew their troops from Vienna in 1955, sparing Vienna from the decades-long Cold War division that split Berlin. To this day, Austria has maintained its policy of neutrality, and has not joined NATO as most other Central European countries now have.
